# Internal Plastering - Frequently Asked Questions

## Services & Products

**What types of plastering services do you offer?**
We provide comprehensive internal plastering services including: new build plasterwork (dot-and-dab on brick/block), renovation and repair work, dry lining systems, ceiling plasterwork, textured finishes (artex, bark, stipple effects), and professional skimming and finishing. Whether it's a single room or a full property, we tailor solutions to your specific needs.

**What's the difference between traditional plastering and dry lining?**
Traditional plastering involves applying wet plaster coats to create finishes and typically costs £30-50/m². Dry lining uses plasterboard fixed to studs or dot-and-dabbed to walls, costing £12-20/m² and offering faster installation and good insulation properties. Plastering offers superior soundproofing and can achieve more varied decorative finishes. Dry lining is more cost-effective for some applications and faster to install.

**Can you work on listed properties or heritage buildings?**
Yes, we have extensive experience with heritage and conservation-listed properties. We use traditional lime-based plasters that are breathable and respect building character and integrity. These finishes are ideal for period properties and comply with conservation area requirements. We conduct full assessments to ensure work meets heritage standards.

**Do you plaster over existing plaster?**
Yes, we can plaster over existing plaster if it is sound and well-bonded. We conduct a thorough assessment to identify any hollow areas, delamination, or structural issues. If the existing plaster is loose, damaged, or delaminated, it must be removed first to ensure a quality, long-lasting result.

## Process & Timeline

**How long does internal plastering take?**
Project timelines vary based on scope: New build projects typically take 2-4 weeks depending on property size and complexity. Renovation projects depend on the extent of remedial work required. Base coats need 7-14 days drying before finish coats are applied. Finish coats require a further 3-7 days before decoration can commence. Complex projects or large properties may take longer.

**Do weather conditions affect the work?**
Weather conditions do impact plastering, particularly humidity and temperature. We manage environmental conditions carefully to ensure proper curing. Excessively damp conditions or rapid temperature changes can affect drying times and finish quality. We adjust our schedule and techniques based on weather forecasts to optimize results.

**Do I need to vacate my property during work?**
For most projects, you don't need to vacate. However, we recommend moving furnishings from work areas for protection and to allow our teams efficient access. For large-scale renovations or if you're sensitive to dust, you may prefer temporary accommodation. We'll discuss specific requirements during the survey and quote process.

**How do I prepare my property for the work?**
We provide a detailed preparation checklist. Generally, you'll need to: clear furniture from work areas, protect floors with coverings, remove wall hangings and fixtures, ensure good ventilation, and maintain reasonable ambient temperature (ideally 15-23°C). We'll advise on specific requirements based on your project type.

**Can plastering work happen in winter?**
Yes, but we take additional precautions. Winter conditions (cold temperatures, high humidity) extend drying times and require careful environmental management. We use specialist techniques and ensure adequate heating and ventilation. We'll advise if winter conditions might impact your project timeline or costs.

## Maintenance & Aftercare

**How do I maintain my new plastering?**
New plaster requires minimal maintenance. Key points: allow at least 7-14 days full curing before heavy use or decoration. Apply a "mist coat" (thinned emulsion) as your first paint layer to ensure proper adhesion. Once decorated, clean with a soft cloth and mild detergent as needed. Avoid abrasive cleaners that may damage the finish.

**Can I paint immediately after plastering?**
No, plaster must fully cure before painting. This typically takes 7-14 days depending on plaster type, substrate, and environmental conditions (temperature and humidity). Premature painting traps moisture and may cause paint failure. We recommend applying a mist coat (thinned emulsion) as your first paint layer for optimal results.

**What should I do if I notice cracks?**
Small hairline cracks are often cosmetic and may appear as plaster fully cures. Larger cracks should be assessed. Contact us with photos and details, and we'll arrange an inspection. Minor cracks can be filled with flexible filler and painted over. Larger cracks may indicate underlying issues requiring remedial work—we'll advise on the best solution.

**Can renders be damaged by weather or moisture?**
Well-installed internal plasterwork is highly durable. Interior plaster isn't exposed to weather, but high moisture environments (bathrooms, kitchens) require consideration. We recommend: good ventilation, extractor fans in wet areas, and maintaining appropriate humidity levels. If moisture issues arise, we'll identify and address the root cause.

**Can I paint over the plaster with any paint?**
While most standard paints adhere to plaster, we recommend using quality emulsion paints designed for plasterwork. Moisture-resistant paints are ideal for bathrooms and kitchens. Always apply a mist coat first (thinned emulsion) to ensure proper adhesion and even coverage. Primer-based systems work well on new plaster.
